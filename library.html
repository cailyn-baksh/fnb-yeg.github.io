Zines, Resources and Literature

Some documents useful for people living in so-called Alberta and a list of zines we've distributed.

    The Basics

    Our usual distribution, these can be found at our SOY meals.
    Anarchism: What it is and what it ain't
    Published by the Sprout Anarchist Collective.
    Life Without Law
    An introduction to anarchist politics published by Strangers in a Tangled Wilderness.
    Mutual Aid: Solidarity in Action
    Created by Boise Mutual Aid
    What is Mutual Aid?
    The script of a sub.media video explaining the basics of mutual aid.
    Colonization and Decolonization
    A manual for Indigenous liberation in the 21st century by zigzag
    Know Your Rights Booklet
    A citizen's guide to dealing with the police produced by the CCLA.
    Rally in Support of the Wet'suwet'en

    Zines distributed on Monday, Nov. 22
    Colonization and Decolonization
    A manual for Indigenous liberation in the 21st century by zigzag
    Heal the People, Heal the Land
    A zine published by Unistot'en camp, describing their history and mission.
    Reconciliation is Dead
    A call for revolt after the raid by tawinikay published by aphikono distro.
    Accomplices Not Allies
    Abolishing the ally industrial complex: An Indigenous Perspective from indigenousaction.org
    Fascism and Antifascism: A Decolonial Perspective
    By Enaēmaehkiw Wākecānāpaew Kesīqnaeh
    Settler Sexuality
    Resistance to state-sanctioned violence, reclamation of anti-colonial knowledges and liberation for all: An Indigenous Feminist Zine, from K'é Infoshop in Tségháhoodzání, Dinétah
    Rethinking the Apocalypse: An Indigenous Anti-Futurist Manifesto
    Published by indigenousaction.org
    Indigeneity, Sovereignty, Anarchy: A Dialog With Many Voices
    Interview with Gord Hill and Alan Antliff, published by the Anarchist Developments in Cultural Studies, 2021.1
    An Activist's Guide to Basic First Aid

    10 Steps to Setting Up a Blockade
    Victoria School Walkout

    Zines distributed on Tuesday, Nov. 30th at the walkout at Victoria school in support of survivors of sexual assault.
    Supporting A Survivor of Sexual Assault
    Prepared through a collaborative effort by UBUNTU and Men Against Rape Culture
    F*CK ME: A Guide to Getting Off and Getting It On
    Fill in the blank zine published by Scarlet Teen.
    Let's Talk About Consent Baby
    A short introduction to the topic of consent added to Sprout Distro in 2013.
    NO! Against Adult Supremacy
    A collection of Youth Liberation writing, originally published online by Stinney Distro. Link is to the first of 20 volumes.
    Ask First!
    Resources for Supporters, Survivors, and Perpetrators of Sexual Assault.
    DIY Guide to Preventing Sexual Assault
    It is up to each of us to prevent sexual assault in our community.
    The Broken Teapot
    An essay by anonymous about recovering from trauma.
    Understanding Patriarchy
    An essay by bell hooks.
    Other Resources

    Other useful resources on various topics.
    On Conflict and Consensus
    A manual which describes a model of consensus called "Formal Consensus", which is the foundation for consensus used by many Food Not Bombs groups.
    Community Supports
    A list of the most helpful resources for low-income and unhoused folk in the city of Edmonton. Please don't ever think you're alone. For fire and medical emergencies, dial 911. We do not ever recommend calling the police. If you are not in an emergency but need guidance on services, contact foodnotbombsedmonton@gmail.com or the 211 hotline.
    Gardening 101
    Nicole of Veg in YEG was kind enough to share some of her gardening knowledge with us! Here are some notes from the session.
    The Anarchist Cookbook
    A cookbook for a world free of rulers, repression, and corporate domination.
    Truth and Reconciliation Commission: Calls to Action
    If you belong to or involved in the leadership of a governmental organization, post-secondary institution, the health-care system, the Federation of Law Societies of Canada, a law school, Library and Archives Canada, an archive organization, a Canadian journalism program, a sports organization, the corporate sector of Canada, a church party or are the Pope, here are actions you can take towards reconciliation.
